About me

I investigate how historical demography and natural selection shape genetic diversity in forest trees, with a special focus on the endangered peat-bog pine Pinus uliginosa.

My work combines population genomics, paleo-climatic modelling and conservation genetics to inform sustainable forest management and species-protection programmes.

Research interests

Population genomics

Demographic history, gene flow and local adaptation in conifers under past and future climate change.

Conservation genetics

Genetic rescue strategies for fragmented populations of peat-bog pine and other endangered tree taxa.

Computational pipelines

Developing reproducible workflows for SNP calling, genomic scans and landscape-genetic modelling.
