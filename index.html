About

I am a population geneticist specialising in forest tree species. My research explores the genomic signatures of speciation, hybridisation and local adaptation in European conifers. I combine extensive field sampling with high-throughput sequencing and modern bioinformatic pipelines to disentangle evolutionary processes shaping biodiversity.

Currently, I hold the position of Assistant Professor (adiunkt) at the Faculty of Biology, Adam Mickiewicz University in Poznań, Poland.

Research Interests

  • Speciation & hybridization in plants
  • Conservation & population genomics of conifers
  • Phylogeography and paleo-climatic modelling
  • Bioinformatic pipelines for NGS data analysis
  • Application of genomic tools in forest management
