About me

I am an Assistant Professor (Research) at the Institute of Environmental Biology, Adam Mickiewicz University, Poznań, where I investigate the genomic underpinnings of adaptation, speciation and hybridization in European pines and other forest trees.

My work integrates field sampling, high‑throughput sequencing and computational phylogenomics to understand how demographic history and climate have shaped current diversity, and to inform conservation and forest management strategies.

Research Highlights

  • Genomic landscapes of hybrid zones in Pinus species.
  • Adaptive potential and genomic vulnerability of Pinus sylvestris to climate change.
  • Phylogeography and post‑glacial recolonisation patterns of European conifers.
  • Development of organellar SNP assays for population monitoring.
